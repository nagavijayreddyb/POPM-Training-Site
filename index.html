Exam Strategy & Tips

Battle-tested advice from candidates who passed on the first attempt

01
Focus on High-Weight Domains
Spend ~60% of study time on Domain 1 (Iteration Execution, 30–32%) and Domain 2 (PI Planning Prep, 21–24%). Together they represent over half the exam. Master these first.
02
Know PO vs PM Differences Cold
Multiple questions test PO vs PM authority. Remember: PO = Team Backlog, PM = ART Backlog. PO accepts Stories; PM accepts System increments. Draw a comparison chart and drill it.
03
Think SAFe Mindset, Not Common Sense
Scenario questions have SAFe "right" answers that may differ from your instincts. Always ask: "What would SAFe prescribe?" Trust the framework. When in doubt, choose the most customer-centric, lean-thinking option.
04
Aim 90%+ on Practice Tests
The real exam requires 80%. Aim for 90%+ consistently on practice tests before sitting the real one. This buffer accounts for unfamiliar phrasings or tricky multi-select questions.
05
Memorise All Timeboxes & Numbers
PI = 8–12 weeks. Iteration Planning = 4 hrs or less. Iteration Review = 1–2 hrs. ART = 50–125 people. These exact figures appear frequently. Use the Cheat Sheet tab to drill them.
06
WSJF Calculation is Always Tested
Know WSJF = CoD / Job Size. Know the 3 CoD components: User-Business Value, Time Criticality, Risk Reduction/Opportunity Enablement. Be able to identify the highest-priority item from a WSJF table.
07
Multi-Select: Select ALL Correct
Questions marked [MS] require ALL correct answers for full credit. A partially correct answer scores zero. Watch out for plausible-sounding distractors. Look for patterns — answers from source material almost always have 3–4 correct options.
08
Take the Exam Within 7 Days of Training
Material is freshest right after the course. Schedule the exam within a week of training. Your free attempt is valid for 30 days after course completion — don't waste it by waiting too long.
09
Master the SAFe Big Picture
The SAFe Big Picture diagram is the blueprint of the framework. Know the hierarchy: Portfolio → Solution Train → ART → Team. Know every event, role, and artifact at each level and how they connect.
10
Understand Uncommitted Objectives
A common trap: uncommitted objectives are NOT optional extras. They ARE planned, DO count toward capacity, and exist to improve predictability when confidence is low. This nuance is frequently tested.
11
Know the 3Cs of Stories
Card (written, brief), Conversation (details emerge through discussion with PO), Confirmation (acceptance criteria). Stories are NOT specs — they're placeholders for conversations.
12
Eliminate Clearly Wrong Options First
For each question, eliminate options that are clearly outside SAFe principles first (e.g. "assign to a developer", "mark as done anyway", "ignore dependencies"). This leaves 2 plausible options and improves odds significantly.
