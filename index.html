Exam Strategy & Tips

Sourced from SAFe workbook + candidates who passed first attempt

01
Focus on High-Weight Domains First
D1 (Iteration Execution, 30–32%) + D2 (PI Planning Prep, 21–24%) = over half the exam. Budget ~60% of study time on these two before the others.
02
Know PO vs PM Differences Cold
PO = Team Backlog; PM = ART Backlog. PO accepts Stories; PM accepts System increments. PM creates Vision; POs contribute. This is the #1 most tested distinction.
03
Think SAFe Mindset, Not Common Sense
Scenario questions have SAFe "right" answers that differ from instinct. Always ask "What would the Lean-Agile framework prescribe?" Customer-centric, decentralised decisions usually win.
04
Aim 90%+ on Practice Tests
The real exam needs 80%. Consistently scoring 90%+ in practice buffers against unfamiliar phrasing and tricky multi-select questions on exam day.
05
First Step of Iteration Planning = Establish Capacity
A direct exam question. "What is the FIRST step?" = Establish Capacity. Not story analysis, not goal-setting. This is explicitly stated in the SAFe workbook agenda.
06
WSJF Is Always Tested
WSJF = CoD ÷ Job Size. Three CoD components: User-Business Value + Time Criticality + Risk Reduction/Opportunity Enablement. Know how to rank items from a WSJF table.
07
Multi-Select = ALL Correct Required
No partial credit on multi-select. "Choose two" = exactly two. Eliminate clearly wrong options first, then pick carefully. The exam never gives partial credit for partially right answers.
08
Take Exam Within 7 Days of Training
Material is freshest right after the course. Your free attempt is valid for 30 days after course completion. Don't wait — schedule within a week for best retention.
09
Uncommitted Objectives Are a Trap
They ARE planned. They DO count toward capacity. They exist to improve predictability by flagging low-confidence items. They are NOT optional extras or "nice to haves."
10
Know All 4 SAFe Core Values
Alignment, Transparency, Respect for People, Relentless Improvement. PLUS Program Execution and Built-in Quality are also Core Values. DevOps and Innovation are NOT Core Values.
11
Vision Input = Customer Feedback
"What is one input to the Vision?" = Customer feedback. Not Portfolio Backlog, not Team topologies, not Feature context. Customer feedback drives Vision evolution.
12
System Demo Anti-patterns
Do NOT use test data (use staging). Team demos do NOT replace System Demos. POs should NOT present ALL work. The System Demo integrates ALL teams' features together.
13
Business Owners' Role at PI Planning
Business Owners assign Business Value (BV) scores to PI Objectives. Their primary responsibility is "aligning priorities and PI Planning." They are key ART stakeholders.
14
Built-in Quality = Agile Teams (Not Testers)
"Who ensures quality is built into code?" = Agile Teams collectively. NOT just Testers, NOT just Developers, NOT the PO alone. Built-in Quality is a shared team practice.
15
Confidence Vote Comes After ROAM
PI Planning Day 2 sequence: Planning Adjustments → Team Breakouts → Final Plan Review → ART PI Risks (ROAM) → Confidence Vote → Plan Rework → Retrospective. Memorise this order.
16
PM Develops Preliminary Iteration Goals When?
PRIOR to Iteration Planning — not during, not in backlog refinement. POs develop these based on team's PI commitments and progress made so far in the PI.
